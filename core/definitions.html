Appendix: Canonical TEP Definitions

This appendix states the canonical definitions used throughout the TEP corpus. Every paper in the series adopts these conventions unless an explicit deviation is noted.

A.1 Matter Metric

The TEP framework operates on a single manifold with two metrics. Gravity is described by a Lorentzian metric gμν; matter fields, clocks, and rulers couple to a causal matter metric g̃μν:

g̃μν = A2(φ) gμν + B(φ) ∇μφ ∇νφ

where φ is the time field, A(φ) = exp(βAφ/MPl) is the universal conformal factor, and B(φ) is a small disformal function consistent with multi-messenger constraints.

The conformal sector governs clock rates; the disformal sector governs cone tilts.

A.3 Temporal Shear

The Temporal Shear is the gradient of the conformal factor:

Σμ = ∇μ ln A(φ) = (βA/MPl) ∇μφ

It measures the local rate of change of the conformal clock-rate rescaling and is the source charge for fifth-force-like effects.

A.4 Temporal Topology

The spatial profile and coherence structure of the scalar field φ (equivalently, of ln A). It describes how the scalar field is organised in space and time, including its correlation lengths, domain boundaries, and topological defects. The coherence length λT is the characteristic scale over which the field remains correlated; at smaller separations the field behaves coherently, while at larger scales random-phase superposition suppresses the net observable effect.

A.5 Screening

Screening is the continuous, spatially varying suppression of locally observable Temporal Shear due to the local matter density. It is not a binary on/off switch but a smooth transition governed by the dimensionless screening function:

f(ρ) = 1 / [1 + (ρ/ρc)n]

where ρc is the phenomenological saturation density (calibrated from terrestrial data) and n controls the steepness of the transition. When ρ ≪ ρc, f → 1 (full TEP effect); when ρ ≫ ρc, f → 0 (saturated, A → 1). The transition from 10% to 90% screened typically spans ρ ~ 2–30 g/cm3.

A.6 Observable Response Coefficient

An observable response coefficient, denoted κ with an appropriate subscript (e.g. κCep, κMSP), is a domain-level empirical transfer coefficient that maps a TEP field structure to a specific observable shift in a given experiment. It is not a bare microscopic coupling; it absorbs the combined effect of local screening, instrument bandwidth, calibration conventions, and systematic residuals. Different experiments constrain different response coefficients, and the same underlying βA can manifest through different κ values in different domains.

A.7 Synchronization Holonomy

The residual non-closure of synchronization transport around a closed loop, after subtracting all known GR corrections (Sagnac, gravitomagnetic / Lense–Thirring, Shapiro delay, gravitational redshift, station motion, clock-scale realization, and reference-frame corrections):

Hresid(C) ≡ ∮C (σ̃ − σGR) = &iint;Σ (F̃ − FGR)

This quantity is invariant under admissible synchronization re-gaugings because the matter-frame connection σ̃ and the corresponding GR reference connection σGR shift by the same exact one-form, leaving the residual connection Δσ = σ̃ − σGR unchanged. In the conformal-only limit (B = 0), the scalar contribution to closed-loop transport is generated by the exact one-form d ln A, so ∮C d ln A = 0 and Hresid vanishes at leading order. A non-zero Hresid requires the disformal sector, non-metricity, or other explicitly non-exact transport structure.
