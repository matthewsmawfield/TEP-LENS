Abstract

We present a geometric blind-prediction residual test for potential-dependent temporal propagation using SN Refsdal — the only multiply-imaged supernova with five resolved images and precision time delays. Seven independent lens-modelling teams predicted the SX reappearance delay before the measurement existed; all six non-zero residuals between their predictions and the subsequently observed delay are positive, matching the sign predicted by a log-magnification proxy model for temporal shear. The pre-specified Wilcoxon signed-rank test yields $p = 0.016$ ($2.2\sigma$; blind-only) and $p = 0.0078$ ($2.4\sigma$; all eight models). Signal-energy analysis confirms this is a single-contrast measurement: 99.9% of the predicted signal resides in the S4–SX baseline, with effective dimensionality $D_{\rm eff} \approx 2.0$. The measured coupling ($\alpha_{\rm lens} = -0.055 \pm 0.044$) is calibrated from the same data and thus definitional; the probative content lies entirely in the sign consistency across independent modelling codes. The test is insensitive to the Mass Sheet Degeneracy but remains limited by lens-model uncertainties ($\pm$16–60 d). Hierarchical Bayesian comparison is inconclusive (BF $\approx 1$), consistent with the current precision regime. Reducing model uncertainties to $\sigma < 5$ d would push this test to $>5\sigma$. Pre-registered falsification thresholds are provided for SN 2025wny.

Keywords: gravitational lensing, time delays, Temporal Equivalence Principle, SN Refsdal, blind prediction, Wilcoxon signed-rank test
