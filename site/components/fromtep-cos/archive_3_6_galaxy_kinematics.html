3.5 Galaxy Age Gradients: Spatially-Resolved TEP Test (ARCHIVED)

Status: This section has been archived. The analysis is retained for reference but is not presented as primary evidence due to the fossil probe insensitivity issue (see Section 1.2).

A complementary time-domain test utilizes MaNGA (Mapping Nearby Galaxies at Apache Point Observatory) IFU spectroscopy to measure stellar population gradients within ~8,600 galaxies. Under TEP, galaxy centers (deeper gravitational potentials) should exhibit time-dilation-induced age signatures distinct from outskirts.

Methodology

The analysis employs Pipe3D stellar population fits from the MaNGA survey:

  • Sample: 8,642 galaxies with high-quality stellar population fits
  • Key observables: Light-weighted age gradients (αAge), central velocity dispersion (σ)
  • Data source: mangaPipe3D catalog (SDSS Data Release 18)
  • Script: scripts/steps/step_6_5_manga_spatially_resolved.py

Complementary Test: Light-Weighted vs Mass-Weighted Age Discrepancy

An independent test utilizes the Firefly stellar population synthesis code, which provides both light-weighted (LW) and mass-weighted (MW) stellar ages for MaNGA galaxies. These age measures probe different stellar populations—LW ages are dominated by young, luminous stars, while MW ages reflect the integrated star formation history. Under TEP, the discrepancy between these age measures should correlate with gravitational potential depth:

Definition: ΔAge = log(AgeLW) − log(AgeMW)

TEP Prediction: r(ΔAge, σ) ≠ 0 (Systematic divergence tracking potential)

Correlation r p-value N Status
Raw ΔAge vs σ +0.48 ≈ 0 10,000 Highly significant
Controlled (metallicity-corrected) +0.098 7.3 × 10⁻²³ 10,000 Significant after controls

Critical Limitation: Fossil Probe Insensitivity

Why This Test Cannot Confirm TEP

As detailed in Section 1.2, TEP modifies the instantaneous rate of proper time (dτ/dt), creating two classes of observables:

  • Time-Domain (Rates): HIGH sensitivity — measures present-tense clock rate
  • Fossil (Archaeology): LOW sensitivity — integrates over ~Gyr formation history

The expected TEP differential (~10 kyr over cosmic time) is O(10⁻⁶) of the formation timescale spread (~Gyr) for stellar populations. The observed correlations (r = −0.09 for age gradients, r = −0.60 for central age) are overwhelmingly dominated by standard galaxy formation physics (downsizing, inside-out formation, light-weighting bias) rather than TEP time dilation.

While the correlation directions are consistent with TEP predictions, fossil probes cannot independently distinguish TEP from standard astrophysical processes at the observed precision. This test is therefore retained as a cross-check but not presented as primary evidence.

Verdict: NOT-DISTINGUISHABLE — The observed correlations are directionally consistent with TEP but match standard galaxy formation physics. As fossil probes integrating over Gyr timescales, stellar age gradients cannot separate the O(10⁻⁶) TEP differential from astrophysical systematics. This analysis is retained for reference but archived as non-primary evidence.
